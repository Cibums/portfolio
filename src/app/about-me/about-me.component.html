About me

When I was eleven years old, I made a simple Google search of "How to make a game," Ever since then, that's the path I've been following. When I was twelve, I found the game engine Unity and some Youtubers making online tutorials on how to use the engine. Through this, I learned how to use Unity, Javascript, C#, and, most importantly, learn more stuff without having a Youtuber make a new video. When it was time for me to choose a gymnasium (the Swedish equivalent of high school), I decided on a game development-orientated school, where I continued to learn all about programming, Unity, design, 3d-modeling and so much more.

Professional History

After graduating from the gymnasium, I got my first job as a full-stack software developer at a big casino-game producer, Play'n GO. I have been working here since August 2021, my current position. Here I've gotten more comfortable with front-end web development, including Typescript, Angular, and React, and developing big projects that usually have to handle enormous amounts of data together with other people.

Free Time

Like many developers, I also like to develop smaller side projects outside of work. For me, these projects are primarily games made with Unity. It is, after all, one of my biggest hobbies. I also want to keep myself updated on the latest regarding Unity and its features. Other than that, I also play a lot of video games. Depending on my mood, I prefer competitive FPS games or more casual, sandbox, and automation games. For example, CS:GO, Valorant, Scrap Mechanic, and Minecraft, to only mention a few. I also play chess and poker, and I play the guitar.
